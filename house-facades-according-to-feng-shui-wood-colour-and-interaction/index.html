House facades according to Feng Shui – wood, colour and interaction

zuppasoup.id May 26, 2024 FENG SHUI TOOL

Just like the interior design, the exterior design of a property influences its holistic energetic effect. According to the teachings of Feng Shui, the proportions and shapes of houses reflect those of the body.

The facade of an object represents the skin or the face and – depending on its surface area – has a significant impact on the overall impression. This article shows the essential factors for a wooden facade according to Feng Shui principles.

The skin wants to breathe, the facade too!

The skin is the human organ that covers the largest surface area for sensory perception. Thanks to the associated surface sensitivity, humans can perceive vibrations, pressure, touch and temperatures, for example.

Just as the natural organ is important for maintaining health, houses also need a shell that enables an exchange of energy between the environment and the interior. 

If buildings are over-insulated, this exchange is not possible or only possible to a limited extent. Ideally, the insulation and the design of the house facade are directly included in the planning of the building according to Feng Shui . Materials and constructions as well as colors must be carefully selected.

Good to know: When compared to the human body, windows symbolize the eyes, the front door represents the mouth. The rooms inside the building represent the organs.

 

Wooden facades – use and maintain surfaces effectively

Wood is a popular facade material - not just in Feng Shui. As an element in the theory of harmony, the natural material stands for departure, growth, beginnings, spring, material prosperity and creativity. In order for the element to unfold its positive effect on the facade of buildings, the material should be recognizable and perceptible as such.

  • For example, if a wooden surface is painted in high-gloss white, the wood element cannot achieve its original effect according to Feng Shui.
  • A white painted facade corresponds to the element metal and therefore has a completely different character than the underlying material.
  • Knowledge of what a facade was originally made of is therefore not the only decisive factor for the effect of the building envelope.
  • Visual (seeing), olfactory (smelling) and haptic (feeling) perception are also elementary.

 

An untreated wooden facade retains its wooden character because the material is recognized and perceived as such. In addition to the smell and the color of the wood, the feel plays a role. When you touch the facade, you can feel the warm surface and the characteristic grain.

Wood protection without elemental changes if possible

If the wooden facade is to be protected from external influences such as rain, UV radiation and insects without affecting the perceptible effect of the material, breathable wood protection products such as glazes are a good option.

"In contrast to varnishes, wood protection glazes do not cover the wood surface and therefore also the grain. The wood protection glaze allows the wood to retain its natural appearance and ensures open pores so that the wood surface can continue to breathe . "

says the product information about the effective wood protection glaze from Baufix . The environmentally friendly wood gel from the specialist manufacturer of building protection products based in Heidelberg bears the "Blue Angel" environmental label. The moisture absorbed by the facade wood can be released again thanks to open-pored glazed facade wood - the facade and thus the skin of the building can breathe.

Weathered wood – easy to maintain, but requires intensive planning

A wooden facade without a protective coating develops a grey patina over the years, which gradually becomes darker. Natural wood changes its appearance significantly and this change should be taken into account during planning. The facade structure must be carefully chosen so that the effect remains positive even after years.

 

A significant factor is weathering and the possible color differences associated with it. A canopy or a projection can cause the wooden facade to have different colors on one side of the building. All areas that are less exposed to the weather remain lighter. Poorly positioned joints and slatted structures also contribute to the outer shell looking unsightly after just a few years.

Homeowners who want to save themselves a lot of work in advance make sure that the wooden facade can dry quickly after a downpour when planning the house. This requires forward-looking construction in harmony with the elements and cardinal directions.

 

Charred wood for refined facades

An alternative are pre-weathered wooden boards specifically for facades, which already have an elegant surface when purchased and promise less severe changes. 

An example of this is the wood with Yakisugi treatment , which is sold by the family-run wood producer Nakamoto Forestry from Japan. The wood for facade cladding is preserved by carbonization. In addition to completely black facades, the durable woods are available brushed so that the original wood colors remain visible.

Achieve the desired effect with colours

If a reduction in the wooden surface is desired, the facade can be designed in color. Depending on the color choice, the yin or yang effect of the building can be strengthened. 

If the goal is a Yin-emphasized facade, blue can be a possible color. But be careful: If a strong earth shape of a property meets the water represented by blue, disharmony can result. The reason is that the earth element absorbs water and destroys its power (also known as a collision). Such collisions are consistently avoided in a Feng Shui- compliant design, because otherwise unrest in perception or effect arises.

To achieve a balanced yin-yang character, all factors must be taken into account. The factors here do not just mean materials and colors, but also the residents who live in the property. It is therefore important to take stock and answer the key question of what the new facade paint should achieve.

Including:

  • To which element can the building be assigned based on its shape?
  • How does the house fit into the surroundings?
  • Which element can the environment be assigned to?
  • Should more energy be directed into the house?
  • Is mediation between the environment and the house desired?

When it comes to terraced houses in different colours, it is advisable to carefully consider the interactions. Depending on the colour combinations and house shapes, some houses can benefit from the interaction, while others (and therefore the residents) are negatively influenced.

  • Even rampant plants climbing along the facade or hanging plants reaching down from a balcony into the ground floor windows can significantly detract from the overall impression.
  • In order to direct positive, lively energy into the house, the facade color should not only match the surroundings. With the help of strong accents - for example in the form of a front door in a powerful color - the desired energy flows into the building.

The characters of the residents must also be taken into account when making the color scheme. For example, a homeowner who is born under the Feng Shui element ( Chinese zodiac sign ) of fire will hardly be attracted to a facade that primarily nourishes water elements.

With Feng Shui advice for new buildings, conflicts can be prevented and a pleasant living experience can be promoted right from the start.
