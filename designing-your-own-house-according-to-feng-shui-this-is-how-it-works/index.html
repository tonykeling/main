Designing your own house according to Feng Shui: this is how it works

zuppasoup.id May 15, 2024 ROOMS

Houses that are furnished according to Feng Shui simply appear more pleasant and comfortable the first time you enter a room and seem to be beneficial to human nature.

Even if it is not possible to measure at first glance what exactly it is about Feng Shui that has such an impact on our perception of a room, the  rules of room design that are practiced in Feng Shui have an effect.

By observing these often very complex rules and the clever use of colors , shapes and the correct division of living areas in the house, a lot can be achieved in your own home.

The house floor plan

A fundamental step in designing a property according to Feng Shui is to create the correct layout of the room. This starts with the planning of the property. Here you can make sure that the rooms are suitable when you buy the house.

If you want to find the right property, you have various options, one of which would be to search via  www.gelbeseiten.de . The basic idea of ​​creating spaces for activity and retreats for periods of rest suggests why the Far Eastern philosophy is effective in interior design.

Assigning individual themes to the rooms and using them only for that purpose can be important, for example in order to be able to really concentrate at work or to really relax in the Feng Shui bedroom .

When there are no distracting elements in sight, it is easier to devote yourself to the phase of the day. However, Feng Shui even specifies the floor plan of how exactly the rooms should ideally be divided.

It is not possible to implement the rooms positively in every apartment and in every house, as floor plans are always different.

However, Feng Shui also provides some tricks here to help balance things out if a room is too small or the rooms are not designed to be square or rectangular.

Consider the five elements 

Another essential pillar in Feng Shui concerns the five elements of wood, fire, earth, water and metal.

Each of these elements is assigned different properties, such as colors or cardinal directions. For example, wood is symbolized by shades of green, while the element of fire can be strengthened by red or orange.

Every person is assigned a beneficial element. This can be determined based on the time of birth. There are not only beneficial elements in room design, but also negative elements.

For example, if there are blue decorations or an aquarium in the room that reflect the element of water, this has a nourishing effect on a wood person. On the other hand, metal furniture or silver tones can have a negative effect on a wooden person. There are beneficial and negative colors or elements for each group of elements.

This should be taken into account especially when choosing colors for home furnishings. If you want to know exactly and on your own personal level, you should also look into the Chinese zodiac sign .

 

The life energy Qi 

One concept underlying Feng Shui is that of the life energy Qi. According to the theory of design, life energy flows through our entire world. Places where Qi can flow feel positive and good and promote our well-being.

According to Feng Shui, places where it cannot flow are places that do not promote our energy. Therefore, residents of a household should always strive to ensure that Qi can flow. If you walk through rooms carefully, you can see a lot for yourself.

Where untidy corners catch the eye, worn furniture or even impractical corners are dictated by the shape of the building, the Qi also stalls. On the one hand, it is important to ensure order, but on the other hand, targeted Feng Shui decorative elements and other furniture can help to let the life energy Qi flow again.

Your 10 tips for a successful home

  1. Decluttering and organizing: Before you start your Feng Shui design, make sure you're organized and get rid of unnecessary things. A tidy home creates better energy.
  2. Use the compass school: The compass is your guide in Feng Shui design. It divides your home into nine areas and helps you determine the best place for different elements and colors.
  3. Color design: Use colors that match the individual areas of the bagua. For example, use calming blues in the area of ​​learning or bold reds in the area of ​​passion and love.
  4. Natural materials: Use natural materials such as wood, stone and fabrics to create a connection with nature.
  5. Symbols and Decoration: Use symbols and Feng Shui decorative elements that promote positive energies, such as mirrors, plants, and artwork.
  6. Arrangement of furniture: Arrange your furniture in a way that allows for good energy circulation. This means that you should not block the flow of traffic.
  7. Bedroom design: Pay particular attention to the design of your bedroom, as this is an important space for relaxation and regeneration. Position your bed so that it has a good view of the door, but is not directly in front of it.
  8. Cleaning and maintenance: Keep your home clean and well-maintained as this increases energy quality.
  9. Lighting: Use natural light wherever possible and complement it with mood lighting.
  10. Personal Intention: Remember that Feng Shui is also influenced by your personal intention and attitude. Visualize positive changes and goals as you design your home.
