Designing your Feng Shui Garden

zuppasoup.id June 1, 2024 GARDEN DESIGN

Feng Shui for the house or garden?

GI sit in my garden again and watch the birds and insects on their wonderful journey through nature. Today it will be an article about garden design according to Feng Shui.

Many of my clients want to have either just the garden or just the house aligned according to Feng Shui criteria. However, there is a close connection between the two, because the external environment of the house plays a decisive role in the building.

If it is difficult for anything to grow outside in the garden, you can assume that there is little energy inside the house as well.

The garden plays an essential role in Feng Shui

From here, the energy and Qi are channeled to the house, which ultimately determines its quality in the house. 

But what exactly do I, as a classic Feng Shui consultant , understand by garden design according to Feng Shui?
I am not a gardener. The type of soil and the plants growing in the garden are not decisive for garden design according to Feng Shui .

Of course, plants should grow, otherwise the life energy would not flow. Every country has its own specific plant world . And every soil also requires its own nature. I had to learn that in my garden too.

At the beginning of a garden design according to Feng Shui, I explore the surrounding landscape

Mountains and trees and also neighboring buildings are examined first. Then the garden is examined in particular.

This approach is essential, so here is an overview of the perfect Feng Shui landscape design.

A shelter at the back of the house. A nice, spacious area at the front towards the entrance . And shelter on both sides, right and left of the house.

I call this principle the Feng Shui armchair. You should sit as if you were in a comfortable armchair so that nothing can attack you from behind and you have a wide view of the front. You want to be able to rest your arms on the right and left.

For my blog post, I was inspired by the placement in the individual  rooms. Because every room has a different function, the mirror should always be used consciously.

Your house should be like you

It needs protection from behind and from the sides so that the rising winds do not endanger the house. There should be a spacious area in front of your house that accompanies your guests to the entrance and supplies the house with good Feng Shui energy.

It is important that the mountains or houses to the right and left of the house are sufficiently far away. The protection from the sides should not be too extreme. For example, another building that shades your house and thus seems rather oppressive.
Once you have looked at your external area, we can now turn to garden design according to Feng Shui.


Before I take out the Feng Shui geomancer compass to determine the cardinal directions, I let my gaze wander through the garden.

The garden says a lot about the residents of the house

If the grass is green and fruity and flowers and bushes are growing lushly, the Qi of the place is powerful. If the earth is too moist even though it hasn't rained, the dampness will also be found in the house.

Trees in particular provide a good indication of geomantic fault zones. If fruit trees in particular split, this indicates earth faults.

In Europe, there is an old farmer’s rule regarding thunderstorms: “You should seek out beeches and avoid oaks.”

Lightning often strikes at water vein crossing points

During a thunderstorm, you should try not to stand under an oak tree, because unlike beech trees, oak trees tend to stand in what are known as disturbance zones.
Trees also reveal a lot about the garden and, above all, what is under the ground. If trees grow crookedly or very poorly, this is always an indication of the Qi in the earth.

Insects and some animals are also always an indication of possible negative earth rays. Ants, cats and bees, for example, love places that we humans should avoid. All of these criteria are very informative when choosing a possible building site and also help when deciding on the right place to stay in the garden.

My eye as a Feng Shui consultant looks even deeper:

Does a terrain slope upwards or downwards? This condition influences the behavior of the water when it rains and it immediately becomes clear whether it is possible to build an artificial watercourse.

In ancient China, a Feng Shui garden would have been unthinkable without water. Today, garden design according to Feng Shui looks a little different. On the one hand, a garden pond can be a source of danger for small children in the house. On the other hand, a watercourse in a Feng Shui garden is beautiful but requires a lot of maintenance.

You can follow a few simple rules for water placement

Water to the right and left of your entrance is also not beneficial to the energy.

This formation is called “crying water” and indicates many tears that will flow in the house.

Likewise, your pond should not be planned exactly behind the house

Stability should be provided by a mountain, hill or elevation. A pond to the left behind the house would be possible, however.

In traditional Feng Shui there are many secret formulas that show which direction a water flow should have. Whether the water flows from right to left or from left to right is extremely important. The orientation of the entrance door is crucial here. The directions of the door are measured according to 24 possible directions and the corresponding Feng Shui water formula is used depending on the orientation. This requires a lot of experience and a good geomantic compass .

It seems easier to create a pond according to Feng Shui

But here too, inflows and outflows or a waterfall could stimulate the Qi. Water in the wrong place can lead to misfortune. Increased accidents, fraud and loss of money are often related to the stimulation of the wrong Qi.

The Feng Shui route to the house is simpler

A winding, meandering path leads the Qi to the house in a harmonious way. Here, too, there are formulas to be read on my Feng Shui compass . The cardinal directions are the decisive factor. Each house has its own orientation and therefore its own formula. This indicates exactly where the path to the house should start and how the path around the house should be.

A simple Feng Shui rule for routing is not to make the path to the house too long or too narrow. This will speed up the Qi and cause unrest and discomfort in the house.

The garden gate

When choosing a garden gate, it is important to remember that the passage is wide. Plants that block the passage should be cut back or even moved.

The garden fence

Garden fences should also be chosen carefully. Pointed and angular shapes, especially if they resemble a spear, indicate what is known as Sha Qi. This negative shape is seen as a harbinger of bad luck. The harmonious image of a hedge would be more favorable instead. Hedges made of bamboo deserve special attention, as they are considered to be harbingers of good luck. Bamboo grows luxuriantly and its leaves are soft in shape.

Planning a garden according to Feng Shui is a lot of fun and also has a positive effect on the neighbors.
