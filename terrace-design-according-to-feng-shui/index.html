Terrace design according to Feng Shui

zuppasoup.id April 25, 2024 GARDEN DESIGN

As a theory of harmony, Feng Shui can   be used not only in building houses , as part of interior design and  in garden design . The principles can also be used to promote the flow of energy on the terrace.

In the flow: How Feng Shui works on the terrace

Feng Shui is always about arranging the five natural elements - symbolized by objects - correctly. On the terrace, these can be a wide variety of items, from comfortable  rattan seating groups  to plant pots and boxes to decorative items.

However, you not only have to align objects outdoors according to Feng Shui recommendations, but you also have to take the proximity to nature into account. This means that surrounding plants must also be selected according to Feng Shui principles for optimal energy flow.

As connecting elements to nature, plants do justice to a fundamental aspect of the teaching. They not only clean the air, but also promote the flow of energy.

Instead of one-sidedness such as just lawn, varied planting is preferable. Feng Shui explicitly recommends plants such as money trees and smaller fruit trees for the terrace area.

To convey positive feelings, they should bloom as brightly as possible.

If plants are located on or adjacent to the terrace, they also need to be looked after. Under no circumstances should they dry out, otherwise they represent one of the most negative elements of the Feng Shui philosophy. In addition to good water drainage, appropriate irrigation can prevent this. If something does dry out, it should be removed from the patio area as quickly as possible.

1. Colors and light: What is important when designing a terrace according to Feng Shui

According to Feng Shui teachings, light colors support the flow of energy more than dark shades. Tones such as light blue, orange, yellow and pastel colors are therefore recommended for furniture and decorative items on the terrace.

Black and brown, like other dark colors, tend to disrupt harmony. You should keep this in mind, especially when choosing garden furniture.

There are always dark color nuances in the area, from the brown of the earth to dark colored leaves in autumn. However, they should be avoided directly on the terrace or in the immediate vicinity.

Just like bright colors, Feng Shui also values ​​natural light positively. Ideally, the terrace is aimed at the most illuminated point in the area, which is probably the sun outside. When it comes to roofing, attention should also be paid to light permeability. Pergolas with climbing plants are suitable because the light is not completely blocked by the leaves of the plants. The natural roof also provides wind protection, which is important according to Feng Shui.

Feng Shui expert tip: The right amount of light

When sunlight falls, there should be the same brightness everywhere on the terrace. Nowhere should it be too light or too dark. If areas are permanently in shadow, it is best to install lamps or candles there. This is not only good for the energy flow, but also ensures relaxation.

2. Balance of elements: What this principle means for the terrace

According to the teachings of Feng Shui, the five elements have meaning both in closed rooms and in open areas such as the terrace.

Because the harmony between wood, earth, metal, water and fire influences how energy manifests itself in the world. If all the elements are present, they harmonize their surroundings.

With this goal in mind, they should also appear in a balanced manner on the terrace or at least be symbolized. can be represented

  • the wood associated with warmth and spring, for example through trees, decking or furniture.
  • the water associated with change and movement, for example through fountains, bird baths, pools or ponds.
  • the earth, which is associated with stable energies, through plant pots, among other things.
  • the metal, which is associated with discipline, for example through decorative elements such as table decorations, chimes or wind chimes.
  • the fire associated with power, for example through candles, torches, triangular symbols or fireplaces.

3. Openness: How the choice of shape on the terrace promotes a healthy flow of energy

The theory of shapes in Feng Shui generally advises against sharp-edged objects and objects with right angles.

Instead, curved shapes that appear soft and promote the flow of energy are recommended. Garden tools such as spades, shovels and scissors should therefore not be stored on the terrace.

It is also better to replace sharp-edged pieces of furniture with round or oval furniture.

Weatherproof textiles for the outdoor area play   an important role, for example in the form of comfortable blankets, hammocks, upholstery, cushions and carpets. Overall, pieces of furniture should be arranged unevenly so that they do not block energy.

According to Feng Shui, energy cannot move freely even on cluttered terraces. Broken, dysfunctional and useless items should definitely be removed. They symbolize a lack of life, which can have a negative impact on terrace guests.

Final tip for patio design according to Feng Shui

Feng Shui principles also play a role in the orientation of the dining area as well as the loungers and seats. Basically, the wall should always be at your back if possible and in this way can provide protection.
