Chinese vases in Feng Shui

zuppasoup.id April 26, 2024 LIFESTYLE

China is a country characterized by various traditions. In addition to the most important holiday,  Chinese New Year,  the People's Republic is also known for its Feng Shui.

A theory of harmony that pursues the harmonization of people with their environment and is the central theme of our training and further education.

The term Feng Shui means something like “wind and water”. This harmonization occurs through the conscious design of the environment in order to bring it into harmony with the people. Antique vases can therefore also play a role in Feng Shui.

What's the deal with ancient Chinese vases?

The theory of harmony not only shapes residential design, but also the planning of gravesites and Chinese garden art.

Chinese vases are often used here. As is well known, these are available in different designs: These include meter-high floor vases, but also bulbous table vases. In China, people made the first pottery vessels in the south of the country over 20,000 years ago. This laid the foundation for today's pottery. Researchers found the oldest ceramic shards in a cave in  Jiangxi Province .

Even today,  ancient Chinese vases still exist  in various shapes, colors and materials.

China now looks back on a 5,000-year-old tradition: During this period, a large number of high-quality antique Chinese vases made of porcelain, silver or bronze were created. 

Anyone planning a trip to China should find out about customs regulations before exporting antiques. According to the  Federal Foreign Office,  antiques are subject to strict regulations.

Some of these works must bear the red seal of the Chinese Cultural Administration. The extent to which this applies to antique Chinese vases must be clarified in advance with the relevant authorities so that there are no unpleasant surprises in an emergency.

What role do (Chinese) vases play in classic Feng Shui?

Vases have various functions in Feng Shui. This is how the tradition of the wealth vase exists.

This is about improving your own prosperity. The Chinese fill this vase of wealth with pyrite or gold coins. If you don't have gold coins on hand, you can also fill the ancient Chinese vases with other valuable items.

 Plants play an important role in traditional Chinese Feng Shui: Among other things, they purify the air, as we have already discussed in more detail in another  blog post .

Because of this, antique vases are suitable for storing these plants. Vases also have other meanings in Feng Shui. Crystal vases, for example, enhance the elemental effect of water due to the metallic energy that emanates from them (cf. Feng Shui plants bring harmony into the home).

What are famous Chinese vases?

The People's Republic of China is known for its Chinese porcelain. These include the antique Ming vases.

The first pieces were created long before the time of the Ming Emperor Yongele (1403 - 1424). These porcelain pieces often stand out because of their blue and white design.

The design of these porcelain vases can be characterized by geometric or floral motifs, among other things. The Ming vase is therefore particularly well known outside of China.

But it's not just Chinese Ming vases that are richly decorated. Other Chinese vases also feature motifs such as dragons, flowers, dogs, etc. and can be found among collectors.
