Feng Shui Travel

zuppasoup.id May 28, 2024 TRAVEL

It can sometimes be difficult to keep your energy balanced while traveling. Fortunately, these days it is becoming easier to be in tune with yourself and your temporary vacation environment, even while on vacation.

For example, there are hotels that are built according to Feng Shui principles or that have decorated their hotel rooms according to Feng Shui. In this article, you will learn more about how you can stay balanced on vacation and travel without worry.

What is Feng Shui?

Feng Shui is a 3,000-year-old science that originated in China. The literal translation of Feng Shui is "wind and water." In Chinese culture, these two elements are associated with good health. 

Over the years, the concept of Feng Shui has become a symbol of good luck and prosperity. Feng Shui creates harmony between people and their living environment and makes it possible to balance the energy flows in one's personal living environment. 

This allows you to improve your life in a targeted manner. You achieve this balance by designing your environment so that you are in harmony with the natural energy flows. By applying Feng Shui in your living environment, the energy of a certain room can be balanced in order to give the people in that environment health and happiness.

This science is especially popular in Asian culture, where it is known as Tao, which means "the way." Taoism is the study of nature, and the principles of Feng Shui reflect this as well.

The principles of Feng Shui

Feng Shui uses the five natural elements and studies how they work together to keep each other in balance. These are the elements of wood, earth, fire, metal and water. 

Each of these five elements has its own characteristics, colors and shapes. They can be used in your living environment to enhance the qualities associated with these elements.

Earth

Earth is associated with self-care, nutrition, and setting boundaries. Earth elements have earthy colors like brown, yellow, orange, and ochre and are often heavy and square. If you want to use an earth element in your home, you can, for example, place a square or rectangular rug or a large and heavy desk in the room.

Water

Water is the element of connection with others and wisdom. This element is associated with colors such as dark blue and black and has wavy shapes. You can use this element by placing a water fountain in your home or by hanging a painting with wavy or water images.

Metal

Metal is characterized by beauty, accuracy and precision. The colors associated with metal are white, gray and metallic colors. Metal elements have a round shape. To use this element in your home, you can use metal lamps or round metal plant pots.

Fire

Fire is usually associated with visibility, inspiration and passion. Fire is associated with the colors red and bright rose and is expressed in triangular shapes. By experimenting with lighting, you can use the element of fire in your home.

Wood

Wood is the element of vitality, growth and healing. The colors of the wood element are green, blue-green and blue, and the objects are often large and column-shaped. The best way to bring the wood element into the home is with plants.

Applying Feng Shui while traveling

It is certainly easy to apply Feng Shui in your own home, but it is a different story when you are travelling. How can you ensure that everything is in balance when you are travelling? 

First, you need to consider the cardinal directions. When determining favorable or unfavorable travel directions in terms of Feng Shui, it depends on which direction you are traveling from and of course where you are traveling. Therefore, concentrate on your cardinal directions as soon as you 

have arrived at your destination. If possible, try to take a detour along the way so that you can concentrate on the direction that is best for you.

You don't need much to manifest a trip. To awaken your travel Qi, you can, for example, visualize your trip. To strengthen your travel Qi even more, you could put up photos of your travel destination, especially in the north sector of your house or apartment.

When you travel and are away from your usual environment, it is important to protect yourself from negative energy. For example, lock your suitcase with a TSA-certified padlock, tie a red ribbon around the handle of your suitcase and take a red scarf with you to wear while traveling. The red color is more noticeable and also protects you from theft.

Energize your hotel room with Feng Shui

Once you arrive at your hotel room, it can be difficult to set it up the way you want. Hotel rooms are visited by many people and the energy of these people can linger in the room for a long time. 

You can remedy this somewhat by carrying your own personal lucky symbol with you. A photo of a Buddha or your personal spiritual guide offers protection. 

Good thoughts simplify interaction with other people during the trip and provide a feeling of satisfaction and harmony. 

You can also freshen up your hotel room with a candle with a slightly calming scent like lavender to brighten the energy in the room and give it a dose of yang energy. Incense should be used discreetly in the hotel room so as not to set off the fire alarm. On the other hand, scented oils and scented sprays are ideal for creating an immediate feeling of arrival.

Leave in peace and apply for a visa, my personal tip

To ensure you can leave with peace of mind, you should make sure in advance that you have all the necessary travel documents. For example, if you are travelling to New York, you will need an ESTA USA . 

This is an electronic travel authorization that is digitally linked to your passport, eliminating the need to go to an embassy or consulate to apply for a physical visa. Once granted, an ESTA is valid for two years and allows multiple stays of up to three months in the US. However, it is important that you carefully check in advance whether you meet the entry requirements for the US and the requirements for the ESTA .
