The Feng Shui kitchen – pleasure for the senses!

zuppasoup.id May 29, 2024 KITCHEN

There are things that affect us both positively and negatively when we get up in the morning. Contrary to popular belief, it is not just the fact that we got up on the wrong side of the bed. Or the various tasks, even unpleasant ones, that we still have to do. Maybe the conversation with our children's teacher . The workload that we have ahead of us.

All of this is not what brings down the mood or makes the still tired head wobble. It is the temporal change in the daily changing Qi quality (energy values).

One of these energy influences is, to give just one example, that of the moon. The moon causes the tides on Earth and thus sets huge masses of water in motion every day and is therefore one of the “phenomena” that we feel every day.

By now everyone knows that Feng Shui has something to do with China.

There are enough books that want to teach us more about Feng Shui .
However, reading books is the wrong approach if I want to learn about Feng Shui in depth. Many books on the European market deal with this science in a very superficial way. Especially if I want to learn the teachings of Feng Shui .

These metaphysical studies, which are thousands of years old, were passed on from grand masters to students. The environmental theory can also be found in European culture, in old churches and monasteries. Churches were built in places of particularly strong power anyway. This gives an idea of ​​how highly developed architecture and landscape observation were and are.

The ancient Chinese science is used to improve the energy values ​​of living spaces and the people who live there. Questions such as: In which direction do we look? Where and how the rooms and furnishings should be placed can be calculated using numerous Feng Shui formulas .

How do I plan a Feng Shui kitchen?

This is a question I am often asked, and there are already “ready-made Feng Shui kitchens” on the market. But how does the kitchen planner know how and where the Feng Shui kitchen is placed in the house ?

Do interior designers or kitchen planners ask you whether your kitchen is located in the north of the house or in the northeast?

Do they also ask for the birth date or the exact degree of the house alignment? These are used as the basis for the associated calculation of the Qi qualities (energy values).

It becomes clear that the whole thing is not so simple after all. There are many other factors that need to be considered, measured and calculated before the Feng Shui consultant is finally ready to say: “This is where the stove goes” or “This would be the best place for your Feng Shui kitchen”!

Feng Shui?

Some tips from the so-called "Esoteric Feng Shui" or "American New Age Feng Shui". Books like "Cleaning Up and Decluttering" are great tips for getting your household back in shape and making some changes.

But this has little to do with Feng Shui.

It is also often explained that decorative mobiles should be hung in front of the window so that the Qi does not immediately disappear through the window. Mobiles are nice to look at, but unfortunately they have no Feng Shui basis whatsoever . 

This aspect would now lead to the question of whether Qi is wind or light? Unfortunately, this view has nothing to do with "classic Feng Shui", even if it is said that faith moves mountains. In order to really achieve the effects of Feng Shui, however, a precise analysis of a  Feng Shui consultation is required .

Why is so much attention paid to the kitchen?

The Feng Shui kitchen is the place where food and nourishment for our body is prepared. This is also the place where the element of fire is usually present - the most active element in Feng Shui teachings.

The elements consist of fire, earth, metal, water and wood. When we cook, we need fire.

In traditional Feng Shui, the kitchen symbolizes the prosperity and health of the family.

Why is the position of the stove so important?

Ideally, the kitchen and the fire of the stove are placed in such a way that unfavorable and negative Qi in the room is suppressed or, better said, burned. The arrangement of the kitchen in the house as well as the direction of the kitchen door and the stove are important factors in a Feng Shui consultation. While we suppress unfavorable Qi, we try to activate auspicious Qi at the same time.

So we want to get good Qi in the Feng Shui kitchen and at the same time the kitchen should be in a bad sector that suppresses the Qi. To do this, the kitchen door should be placed in one of the good directions to collect good Qi in the room.

The direction of the stove should also "face" in a good direction. But what is the direction of the stove? In Chinese, that is "the mouth of the fire". But nowadays there is no longer an open flame on the stove, so the front of the oven or stove is considered a reference and orientation point.

How do we find out our favorable and unfavorable directions and sectors?

The directions are based on the "Life Gua" which is calculated from the date of birth (see table below). Each person has four auspicious and four unauspicious directions. For example, a male person born in 1963 belongs to Gua 1 (KAN 1). Ideally, the kitchen for this person should be placed in the southwest and the stove and oven control buttons should face southeast.

And what if different people have different good directions?

The Feng Shui masters in Far Eastern culture agree on this. The focus of the alignment falls on the "breadwinner" of the house. In old Chinese houses, you often find two cooking areas. In fact, people used to cook here for people from the East and West groups (East group Gua 1 / 9 / 3 / 4 - West group Gua 2 / 8 / 7 / 6).

Further tips for kitchen planning:

  • It should be avoided to place the kitchen in the North West sector of the house. If the kitchen is in this sector of the house, it can make it difficult for the man to earn money and his career.
  • No toilet door that opens into the kitchen
  • Avoid bedrooms above the kitchen
  • When cooking, it would be advantageous to have the kitchen door in view
  • The shape of the kitchen, whether round or angular, does not play a major role in Feng Shui . Taste is paramount here.
