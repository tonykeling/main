Renovation work according to Feng Shui: How can you finance it?

zuppasoup.id April 27, 2024 RENOVATION

Feng shui is not just about coordinating the interior design of a room.
The Far Eastern furnishing technology sometimes comes into play in the floor plan. A  Feng Shui renovation  can include smaller, simple measures that can achieve a lot.

There are also more complex renovation measures that involve planning a room from scratch or even changing the floor plan. However, such measures are not entirely cost-effective and require financing. The following article explains how this can be achieved.

What does renovating mean?

Renovation means changing the basic appearance of a house. This includes painting and painting in certain colors, repairing furniture or furnishings and refinishing floor coverings.

The aim is to improve the visual condition of the property in order to feel more comfortable in it and, if necessary, to be able to sell it more profitably later.

In the spirit of Feng Shui, renovating can be used  to  improve the quality of life at home . The term modernization is often used in the same context.

Modernization is about bringing the property up to date. This means, for example, energy-saving heaters or new, well-insulating windows are installed.

While you can usually carry out renovation work yourself at home, extensive modernizations usually require the help of specialist personnel. Accordingly, they are also significantly more expensive.

Renovation and conversion work in the sense of Feng Shui could include the following things:

  • Plan rooms from scratch, taking into account cardinal points and interference zones, for example
  • Change and redefine the function of the rooms, tailored to lighting conditions, cardinal directions and expanded Feng Shui principles
  • Adjust furnishings accordingly, rearrange or buy new ones
  • Pull in or tear down walls to connect rooms or create smaller spaces
  • Install or reglaze windows to improve energy efficiency and lighting conditions

Basically, the more you know in advance about planned renovation work, the better you can calculate the costs that will arise later. These in turn influence which types of financing are suitable.

How much does a Feng Shui renovation cost?

It is not possible to say in general terms how much it will cost to renovate your living space, as the costs depend on many factors:

  • How large is the living space that needs to be changed?
  • How much is done in-house and how much is given to specialist staff?
  • How extensive are the renovations?

The basic value for a comprehensive renovation of a house should be around 600 euros per square meter.
This means that for an area of ​​100 square meters it costs around 60,000 euros.

Due to their smaller size, apartments are usually a little cheaper to renovate, so you can expect to spend around 500 euros per square meter.
The good thing, however, is that the need for renovation doesn't happen overnight. You usually know long in advance that something needs to be done and can prepare accordingly financially.

How do you finance the renovation?

Fortunately, there are a number of options for financing renovation work. One variant is   to use a building savings contract for renovations .

In such a building savings contract, you save over the years and on favorable terms until you finally have it paid out for a specific project. If the renovation costs are too high for the contract concluded, you may need to take out an additional loan.

There are three different options: the housing loan, the modernization loan and the installment loan.

Home loans are an uncomplicated financing option

To get this loan, you just need to prove that you own a property that the loan will be used for. A sum of between 5,000 and 50,000 euros can then be financed with such a loan.

A modernization loan, on the other hand, is usually used when the renovation becomes more expensive, as higher amounts are possible. More expensive renovation work can be made possible with such a modernization loan through construction financing.

This is a little more complicated because it is secured by a land register entry. But it offers even lower interest rates than the home loan. A solution that is uncomplicated, but less favorable in terms of interest rates, is the free installment loan. This loan is not tied to any purpose. Amounts between 1,000 and 50,000 euros can be financed.

However, due to the higher interest rates, this alternative is rarely worthwhile for property owners.

Finally, you sometimes also have the opportunity to take advantage of government funding. This is an option for projects that aim to increase energy efficiency.

The BAFA (Federal Office of Economics and Export Control), for example, provides  subsidies of up to 45 percent  of the eligible costs. These grants do not have to be repaid.

An alternative is KfW funding from the Kreditanstalt für Wiederaufbau. Cheap development loans can be taken out through KfW, which can be used for sustainable construction projects.
