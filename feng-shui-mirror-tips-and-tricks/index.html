Feng Shui Mirror: Tips and Tricks

zuppasoup.id May 30, 2024 INTERIOR DESIGN

No other home accessory is as hotly debated as the mirror in Feng Shui. In almost every home consultation I am asked:

“Where should the mirror be placed in Feng Shui?”

I know from experience from the many Feng Shui consultations I have given to many of my clients how sensitive this topic is. I wonder whether it is our own image that is reflected and prompts us to look for a well-chosen place.

Anyone who enjoys observing themselves knows that not every reflection looks benevolent.

The angle and the light play an important role in how we look. If the light is too bright, many  wrinkles become  visible and the angle determines whether a silhouette is thick or thin. Individual purchasing decisions can depend on the placement of the mirror. The viewer wants to see themselves as pretty and attractive.

For my blog post, I was inspired by the placement in the individual rooms . Because every room has a different function, the mirror should always be used consciously.

This is what you should know about mirrors in Feng Shui.

A very important criterion for the correct placement is what is reflected in the mirror. Avoid all negative features that you see in it. These can be sharp, broken and pointed objects. Corners and edges of wall projections should also be avoided. The garbage can and other musty and broken things should not be visible in it.

Let's walk through the apartment and look at it room by room.

1. Entrance and hallway area

You've probably experienced this when you enter a room and back away because you think someone is already there. In the end, it's just your own reflection that looks back at you. There shouldn't be a mirror opposite the entrance . The good Qi is reflected and immediately retreats outside.

2 Bedrooms

The Feng Shui bedroom should appear calm and harmonious. In Feng Shui, any reflection is an activator of Yang energy. Avoid mirrors that reflect the bed . Large mirror surfaces on the cupboards and walls cause unrest.

With a mirror, the bed and the people sleeping in it are doubled. Symbolically, you are no longer alone with your partner in the room. Marital problems can already become apparent here.

Since most bedrooms also have a closet, a mirror is desirable. This could be attached to the inside of the closet door. When the closet is closed, there is no reflection.

This was often the case with old farmhouse cupboards. Even chests of drawers with mirrors used to have a folding function so that the mirror could be folded away again at night.

An old Chinese saying goes that the soul travels in the evening and when it returns it is frightened by the mirror. This means that part of the soul may not arrive.
It is also said that we free ourselves of energetic waste during sleep. But this cannot happen if the discharged energy is reflected back.

An old proverb in China says that the soul goes on a journey in the evening and when it returns, it would be frightened in the mirror.

3. Living room

Mirrors should also be avoided in the seating area of ​​the Feng Shui living room  . It is extremely unpleasant to keep seeing ourselves in the mirror while we are sitting on the couch. It can also disrupt a pleasant conversation with our visitors. The viewer will feel like they are being watched by their own reflection in the mirror and will focus on their own image.

Experience report

I can still remember a time when I was asked to go into the living room for a consultation . There was a table with several chairs and on the opposite side there was a wall-sized mirror. I could look at myself from top to bottom. My client didn't notice this.

He was used to always seeing his reflection in the mirror. But for me it was a strange situation because I didn't feel comfortable with it.

I had the feeling that there was another person in the room . I couldn't imagine sitting on the couch that was also there and relaxing. This room was filled with too much Yang energy. This made my customer restless and lacking in energy. As Feng Shui consultants, we always try to create a balance. Too much Yang can be just as exhausting as too much Yin energy.

4. Kitchen

If the stove is located opposite the main entrance, in Feng Shui we speak of the tiger opening its mouth. This is not desirable, as it can bring losses to the household. A well-placed mirror can help here. This way the Qi of the stove can be directed away from the kitchen entrance without escaping.

If the dining area is in the Feng Shui kitchen , it is not desirable to have a mirror there. This is because we use many objects in the kitchen such as knives, scissors and other sharp utensils. These should not be reflected under any circumstances, otherwise the risk of accidents increases.

5. Dining room

The table on which the food is served should be doubled. A mirror would be ideal here. It means wealth , double happiness and that food is available in abundance. However, care should be taken to ensure that it is a beautiful sight opposite the mirror. A mirror is more than just a Feng Shui decoration .

6. Children's and youth rooms

There should be no mirrors in the Feng Shui children's room , as they cause unrest and turbulence. A child's room needs peace and security; too many objects, such as toys and school supplies, would double up in the mirror. This can create chaos.

7. Office

In Feng Shui, a mirror should not be placed opposite the  workplace  . If the Feng Shui office is a very small room , then a well-placed mirror can of course make it appear larger. However, the direct field of vision at the workplace should not have a mirrored wall.

It is beneficial to enter the  room  with full awareness . Some objects are no longer noticed. They have always been in that place. Only things that we pay attention to come back into our consciousness.

This is how many unfavorable factors creep in and affect our lives. Ultimately, the room, every object and every person affects us. By consciously perceiving and understanding how things work, you have control over them in Feng Shui .

Observe yourself. See how you are and feel inside yourself. Then you will find out for yourself where the right place for the mirror is.
