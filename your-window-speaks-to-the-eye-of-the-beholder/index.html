Your window speaks to the eye of the beholder

zuppasoup.id May 22, 2024 RESIDE

Feng Shui brings positive energy and harmony into the living space. There is a lot to consider when designing your own four walls according to the interior design concept. Not only furniture and decoration are taken into account.

The windows also play an important role. In this article I will explain the principles of Feng Shui. You will also learn how to design your windows according to Feng Shui.

Windows are the eyes of your house; it doesn’t take much to see the world from a different perspective!

The principle behind Feng Shui

Feng Shui is a Chinese theory of harmony. It aims to create a balanced and harmonious environment. 

The principle behind it is based on the idea that the natural environment and the energy it radiates have a direct influence on our well-being and our life situations. 

By applying Feng Shui techniques, we can ensure that our home and work environment radiates positive energy and balance. 

Careful placement of furniture, use of certain colors and integration of natural materials can all help maintain Qi and therefore our well-being. 

Feng Shui can not only help to create a comfortable and balanced home, but it also influences our lives in a positive way.

What role do windows play in Feng Shui?

Windows are an important part of Feng Shui practice. They play a significant role in the circulation of energy in the room. 

The goal of Feng Shui is to design a room so that positive energy can flow freely. Windows can help with this by bringing fresh air and natural light into the room, thus getting the energy flowing. 

However, it is important that the windows are in proportion to the other furniture and kept clean to achieve the full potential of Feng Shui. 

In addition, you should not cover the windows with any curtains. A pleated blind in natural tones, for example, is a good option. But that's not all:

The right shape of the windows

In Feng Shui, the proportions of the windows have a special meaning. Tall, rectangular windows have an invigorating effect and bring a breath of fresh air into the room. 

Wide windows, on the other hand, create an atmosphere of stability and comfort. Round or square windows are particularly harmonious and give the room a gentle and balanced ambience. 

By choosing the right shape of the windows, you can not only add an aesthetic touch to the room, but also positively influence the energy and balance in the room.

Curtains, blinds and pleated blinds

Window coverings also play an important role in Feng Shui. Basically, they should not block the natural light and the view outside. 

In addition, they should be made of natural materials such as cotton, silk or linen and blend harmoniously with the colors of the surroundings. 

The way they are installed also plays a role: curtains should be wide and blinds should be narrow so as not to interrupt the energy. Pleated blinds are also particularly suitable. 

These are both functional and aesthetic. They can regulate natural light and thus create a relaxed and calming atmosphere in the room. They can also serve as protection against negative influences as they balance the energy. 

Pleated blinds come in different colors and materials so that they can be adapted to different interior design styles. Clean your windows and you will have a clear view of the future!

 

Further rules

In order to utilize the full potential of Chinese harmony, there are other general rules. For example, the windows should always be kept clean.

Curtains, pleated blinds, etc. should be opened during the day to allow light and fresh air to flow freely. 

Regular ventilation of the room is also very important to keep the energy in the room fresh and vital. It is also advisable not to place any furniture directly in front of the window to keep the view outside clear. With these simple tips you can improve the Feng Shui in your home and create a positive atmosphere.

The history of Feng Shui

Feng Shui is thousands of years old. The history of Feng Shui goes back to ancient China, where this art was kept as a secret by emperors and kings. 

Over time, the knowledge of Feng Shui has developed and spread. Even today, it is still practiced by many people all over the world. 

The basis of Feng Shui is the idea that everything in our lives is interconnected and needs balance to function optimally. 

By applying Feng Shui principles, we can not only make our environment more harmonious, but also improve our own well-being and quality of life.

Feng Shui in the rooms: The basic structures

Although there are many different teachings of Feng Shui, they all share certain fundamentals. 

One of the most important rules is the placement of furniture and objects in the room. Apartments and houses should be designed to create a pleasant atmosphere and make all rooms accessible to the Qi. A qualified Feng Shui consultant will take care of every detail of a living space and try to let the life energy flow gently.

In addition, all items in the home should be placed harmoniously and in a balanced way to create a peaceful and pleasant environment. Light also plays an important role.
